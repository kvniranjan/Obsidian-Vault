### EY: 49% of Global Consumers Now Use AI for Savings and Investment Decisions
**Source:** [EY Global Newsroom](https://www.ey.com/en_gl/newsroom/2026/04/nearly-half-of-global-consumers-now-use-ai-to-guide-savings-and-investment-decisions)  •  **Date:** April 24, 2026

EY's second Global AI Sentiment Survey — 18,152 respondents across 23 countries — found that nearly half of global consumers used AI to support savings and investment decisions in the past six months, a striking shift from near-zero practitioner-level adoption two years ago. Beyond research, 21% have used AI agents for financial product recommendations, 14% have allowed AI to select financial service providers on their behalf, and 11% have deferred to AI to manage their finances with minimal human intervention. Gen Z leads at 68% adoption overall, followed by millennials at 65%. A parallel signal: 50% of respondents believe AI has the potential to detect and prevent financial fraud — a number that suggests consumer readiness for AI-native fraud controls is ahead of most institutions' deployment. For retail banks and wealth managers, the survey defines a rapidly narrowing window: customers are already using competitor AI tools for financial decisions, and the primary risk is not adoption friction but being bypassed entirely by the AI layer that aggregates and acts on financial data.

### Simply Business Becomes the First Insurer in ChatGPT's App Directory
**Source:** [PR Newswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/simply-business-launches-insurance-app-for-small-businesses-in-chatgpt-in-the-us-302750955.html)  •  **Date:** April 23, 2026

Simply Business — a digital SME insurer with over 1 million customers worldwide and a subsidiary of Travelers — launched a business insurance application in ChatGPT's App Directory in both the US and UK, making it the first insurer to establish a distribution presence inside a major AI assistant. The mechanics are deliberately low-friction: a small business owner provides only three data points (business type, estimated annual revenue, and ZIP code) to receive an indicative price within the ChatGPT conversation; they are then routed to Simply Business's website to complete the full quote and purchase process securely. The launch follows the company's October 2025 rollout of a hyper-personalized AI advisor in the US and represents a deliberate distribution strategy: meeting SME owners where they increasingly ask operational questions rather than requiring them to navigate insurance-specific interfaces. For P&C carriers and insurtech competitors, the signal is architectural — ChatGPT is becoming a front door for financial products, and institutions that establish early App Directory presence will capture intent signals at the earliest point in the decision journey before the customer ever visits a traditional quote engine.

### Cytora and LexisNexis Integrate AI-Powered Data Analytics for US Commercial Underwriting
**Source:** [PR Newswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/cytora-and-lexisnexis-risk-solutions-announce-strategic-relationship-to-enhance-risk-selection-and-automation-for-us-commercial-insurers-302750794.html)  •  **Date:** April 23, 2026

Cytora, whose LLM-powered platform digitises incoming risks, evaluates them against configured underwriting rules, and routes them for automated or manual decisioning, announced a strategic integration with LexisNexis Risk Solutions to embed commercial data analytics directly into the underwriting workflow for US commercial insurers. The first capability is LexisNexis Commercial Data Prefill, which automatically augments submitted risks with business firmographics and applies proprietary entity-resolution technology to accurately identify individual business entities — eliminating a manual data-enrichment step that is simultaneously slow and error-prone. The practical effect is that underwriters receive decision-ready risk assets rather than raw submissions requiring research. Cytora COO Juan de Castro framed the ambition explicitly: building "one of the world's most comprehensive data ecosystems for insurers" by integrating additional LexisNexis commercial insurance products into the platform over time. For commercial lines carriers under pressure to accelerate submission triage without sacrificing risk selection quality, the partnership addresses the key friction point — data completeness at submission — that determines whether AI-driven underwriting automation is commercially viable or simply shifts the bottleneck downstream.

### Taiwan's 16-Bank Alliance Launches Sovereign Financial AI Model to Break Foreign Dependency
**Source:** [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-23/taiwan-banks-to-build-own-ai-model-to-rival-global-giants)  •  **Date:** April 23, 2026

The Taiwan FinTech Alliance — comprising 16 financial institutions including major state-backed banks and leading financial holding companies — announced it will collectively build an open-source-based large language model specifically for Taiwan's financial sector, with training beginning in May 2026, a banking-focused prototype available in Q3, and a full deployment targeting year-end. The strategic rationale is threefold: global models such as ChatGPT, Gemini, and Llama lack embedded knowledge of Taiwan's detailed financial regulations and supervisory practices, creating compliance exposure during audits; the initiative aligns with Taiwan's AI Basic Act (effective January 2026) and the government's "sovereign AI" programme under the Ministry of Digital Affairs; and the consortium explicitly specified the model will not be built on a Chinese platform — a geopolitical constraint as significant as the technical one. Initial use cases are back-office: customer support, document analysis, internal knowledge retrieval. Insurance and securities are targeted for 2027. The Taiwan initiative is a high-visibility instance of a broader pattern emerging globally: national financial sectors concluding that relying on foreign general-purpose AI creates unacceptable regulatory, security, and vendor-concentration risk, and investing collectively in jurisdiction-specific alternatives. For global AI platform providers, it is an early signal that the enterprise financial services market may fragment along regulatory-sovereignty lines faster than currently projected.
