### APRA Tells Banks, Insurers, and Super Funds Their AI Controls Are Behind
**Source:** [APRA](https://www.apra.gov.au/news-and-publications/apra-calls-for-a-step-change-ai-related-risk-management-and-governance) / [APRA industry letter](https://www.apra.gov.au/apra-letter-to-industry-on-artificial-intelligence-ai) / [FinTech Global](https://fintech.global/2026/05/01/australian-regulator-flags-ai-risks-for-financial-firms/) - **Date:** April 30-May 1, 2026

Australia's prudential regulator has put a sharper point on the AI governance problem. APRA's targeted review of large banks, insurers, and superannuation trustees found that governance, risk management, assurance, and operational resilience are not keeping pace with the scale and complexity of AI adoption. The findings are not cosmetic. APRA flagged provider concentration risk, weak contingency planning, embedded AI inside broader software and developer tools, fragmented assurance, board literacy gaps, and information security controls that are moving too slowly. It also warned that frontier AI models, including Anthropic's Claude Mythos, could increase the probability, speed, and scale of cyber attacks by making vulnerability discovery easier for bad actors. The useful lesson for financial services leaders is that regulators are starting to treat AI as an operational resilience issue, not just a model-risk issue. That is the right framing. A bank can have a policy, a committee, and a vendor questionnaire and still be exposed if nobody can explain where AI is embedded, how it changes, who can override it, what happens if a provider fails, and whether cyber controls are keeping up. APRA is not proposing new requirements right now, but that is not a pass. It is a warning shot.

### Agentic Compliance Moves Toward Continuous Financial Crime Operations
**Source:** [FinTech Global](https://fintech.global/2026/05/01/how-agentic-ai-is-reshaping-financial-crime-compliance/) / [SymphonyAI](https://www.symphonyai.com/webinar/from-theory-to-action-ai-agents-transforming-financial-crime-compliance-in-real-time/) - **Date:** May 1, 2026

FinTech Global covered SymphonyAI's push for an "always-on compliance" model using agentic AI to support real-time financial crime detection, investigation, and decisioning. The pitch is that AI agents can automate data gathering, transaction analysis, case summarization, and suspicious activity report preparation, with claimed investigation-time reductions of up to 60%. This is exactly where financial crime compliance is headed, because the old operating model is too slow: periodic reviews, fragmented systems, manual evidence collection, and analyst time wasted on administrative assembly instead of risk judgment. But banks should be blunt about the risk. A faster investigation process is not automatically a better one. If an agent misses context, over-compresses evidence, or buries uncertainty in a polished case summary, the compliance team inherits a confident-looking control failure. The right adoption pattern is narrow and auditable: use agents to assemble evidence, propose next steps, and draft structured case materials, while preserving source links, confidence markers, human review, and management reporting. Compliance AI should make investigators harder to fool, not easier to bypass.

### ACI and Kinexys Push Payee Verification Into Real-Time Payment Flows
**Source:** [ACI Worldwide / Nasdaq](https://www.nasdaq.com/press-release/aci-worldwide-and-kinexys-jp-morgan-collaborate-mitigate-global-real-time-payments) / [FF News](https://ffnews.com/newsarticle/paytech/aci-worldwide-and-kinexys-by-j-p-morgan-collaborate-to-mitigate-global-real-time-payments-fraud/) - **Date:** April 24, 2026

ACI Worldwide and Kinexys by J.P. Morgan are integrating Kinexys Liink's Confirm application into ACI's Fraud and Financial Crime solution. The goal is simple and important: validate account and payee information inside the payment workflow before money moves. That matters because real-time payments compress the fraud window to nearly zero. Once an instant payment is sent, post-event monitoring is mostly a damage report. ACI cites authorized push payment scam losses of USD 4.4 billion globally, with losses projected to reach USD 7.6 billion by 2028, and says 63% of those losses already occur on real-time rails. The strategic signal is that payee verification is becoming core payments infrastructure, not an optional fraud add-on. For banks, the hard work is integrating verification consistently across ACH, wire, instant payments, and card-linked account-to-account flows without creating a mess of channel-specific controls. The winners will not be the firms with the flashiest fraud model. They will be the firms that can verify payees, score behavior, enforce controls, and explain decisions before irrevocable settlement.

### Q2 Adds Real-Time Account Takeover Controls for Digital Banking
**Source:** [FinTech Global](https://fintech.global/2026/04/28/q2-adds-real-time-fraud-detection-capabilities/) - **Date:** April 28, 2026

Q2 launched two AI-supported fraud protection capabilities for financial institutions: User Activity Monitoring and Restricted Entitlements Mode. UAM analyzes behavioral signals during live digital banking sessions to detect high-risk activity, while REM acts as an enforcement layer that can limit permissions or contain compromised accounts in real time. The product detail matters less than the architecture. Account takeover is no longer a problem that can be handled after login or after a suspicious transfer request. Fraudsters move through authenticated sessions, exploit social engineering, and try to complete actions before manual review can react. Banks need session-level monitoring and control, not just stronger front-door authentication. The practical takeaway is direct: detection without enforcement is weak. If a digital banking platform can spot risk but cannot instantly restrict capabilities, step up authentication, pause specific actions, or preserve evidence, the model is only half useful. AI fraud tools need to sit close enough to the workflow to change outcomes while the customer is still online.

### Experian and Resistant AI Put APP Fraud Detection on a Bigger Data Stack
**Source:** [Experian / Nasdaq](https://www.nasdaq.com/press-release/experian-launches-ai-powered-transaction-forensics-bolster-fraud-prevention-across-uk) / [FinTech Global](https://fintech.global/2026/04/23/experian-launches-ai-fraud-detection-tool-for-uk-banks/) - **Date:** April 22-23, 2026

Experian has gone live in the UK with Transaction Forensics, an AI-powered fraud and AML solution developed with Resistant AI. The product combines Resistant AI's behavioral and transaction analytics with Experian's consumer and commercial data, using more than 80 AI models across bank-to-bank payments including Faster Payments, BACS, and CHAPS. Experian says pilot testing delivered a 200% uplift in authorized push payment fraud detection, an 80% reduction in false positives, and a 50% reduction in alert volumes. Those are vendor-reported figures, so banks should test them hard. Still, the direction is right. APP fraud, mule activity, and synthetic behavior patterns cannot be handled with narrow transaction rules and isolated customer records. The modern fraud stack needs broader identity, account, behavioral, business, and payment context in real time. The warning for financial institutions is that "AI fraud detection" is not a product category by itself. The quality of the data graph, the ability to reduce false positives, and the operational path from alert to action will decide whether the tool actually protects customers.
