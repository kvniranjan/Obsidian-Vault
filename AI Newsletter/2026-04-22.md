The Bank of England's April 2026 Financial Policy Committee record confirmed that the BoE has moved from passive observation to structured scenario analysis and simulation to evaluate how AI could destabilise the UK financial system. Four primary vulnerability channels are under examination: market herding (firms deploying similar models may react identically during stress, amplifying selloffs); provider concentration (dependence on a small number of AI and cloud vendors creates single-point-of-failure risk); agentic AI in payments and trading (autonomous systems making rapid decisions could trigger unintended systemic consequences); and cyber risk amplification (AI-enhanced attackers scaling breaches across interconnected institutions). The BoE emphasised that advanced AI is not yet creating systemic instability — but warned that risks could "rise quickly" as adoption accelerates. The practical signal for compliance and risk teams: systemic-risk framing is now arriving from the regulator, not just the industry, which means governance, vendor diversification, and AI stress-testing documentation will become supervisory expectations, not voluntary best practice.
