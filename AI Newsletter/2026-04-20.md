### Wealth Management AI: 81% See It as Critical, Only 10.5% Use It Daily
**Source:** [FinTech Global](https://fintech.global/2026/04/17/ai-in-wealth-management-closing-the-implementation-gap/)  •  **Date:** April 17, 2026

A FinTech Global analysis drawing on the AfW Intermediary Barometer and Bitkom data exposed a stark paradox in wealth management AI: 81% of firms regard AI as the most critical technology for their future and 51% believe firms without AI adoption lack long-term viability — yet only 35% of financial intermediaries actively use AI tools and a mere 10.5% do so daily. The barriers are primarily human, not technical: 53% cite lack of technical know-how and 51% point to staff shortages. Fincite identifies three immediate priorities — deploying relationship manager co-pilots and next-best-action tools with built-in explainability (near-term value); building robust wealth data infrastructure with governance and model registries (foundation layer); and leveraging FinTech partnerships where speed-to-value matters without surrendering data control (strategic lever). Notably, the technology gap has closed: it is now a governance and judgment gap, with neobrokers and non-bank providers outpacing traditional wealth managers precisely because legacy systems and internal governance friction delay deployment, not capability. For wealth managers globally, the window between AI adoption and competitive disadvantage is narrowing faster than internal change programmes typically move.
