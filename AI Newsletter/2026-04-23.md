### FCA Selects Barclays, UBS, Lloyds and Five Others for AI Live Testing Second Cohort
**Source:** [Financial Conduct Authority](https://www.fca.org.uk/news/press-releases/fca-announces-second-cohort-ai-live-testing)  •  **Date:** April 22, 2026

The UK's Financial Conduct Authority announced the eight firms admitted to the second cohort of its AI Live Testing programme: Barclays, UBS, Lloyds Banking Group (Scottish Widows), Experian, GoCardless, Coadjute, Aereve, and Palindrome. Testing — conducted in partnership with AI assurance specialist Advai — begins in April and runs through year-end, with an evaluation report due in Q1 2027. Use cases under supervised test include AI-driven investment support, consumer credit insights, agentic payments, AML detection, and KYC automation. The announcement matters for the entire sector because the FCA's approach — structured sandbox testing with real firms on live use cases — is generating the regulatory evidence base that will shape future AI guidance across UK financial services. Unlike prohibitionist postures, this model explicitly tests "good and bad" AI outcomes under regulatory observation, giving participants a governance head start and the FCA concrete data on how AI actually performs in production-grade banking and insurance contexts.

### Backbase Launches AI-Native Banking OS — A New "Above-Core" Layer for Agentic Banking
**Source:** [FF News](https://ffnews.com/newsarticle/fintech/backbase-launches-the-ai-native-banking-os-defining-a-new-category-for-agentic-banking)  •  **Date:** April 23, 2026

Backbase launched what it calls the AI-Native Banking OS on April 23 — a unified operating system that sits above a bank's existing core systems (cores, payments, cards, risk, CRM) without replacing them. The platform introduces three integrated layers: an Intelligence Layer that surfaces risk, revenue, and churn signals before they materialise; a Semantic Layer (Nexus) that provides customers, employees, and AI agents with a single consistent view of customer data; and an Authority Layer (Sentinel) that governs what each actor — human or AI — can execute, with full audit logging for regulators. CEO Jouk Pleiter's diagnosis is precise: approximately 80% of frontline banking work happens in the "whitespace" between disconnected systems — exactly the gap where AI agents currently fail or go rogue. The Banking OS's concept of "Elastic Operations" directly addresses the constraint that has blocked many AI deployments: without shared context and explicit authority controls, deploying agents at scale creates as many governance problems as it solves.

### AI Startup Claimable Reverses Thousands of Denied Health Insurance Claims
**Source:** [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/features/2026-04-22/ai-and-mark-cuban-among-startup-s-tools-to-fight-denied-health-care-claims)  •  **Date:** April 22, 2026

Bloomberg profiled Claimable, a startup co-founded by British physician and entrepreneur Warris Bokhari that uses AI to help patients appeal denied health insurance coverage decisions — reversing thousands of denials for treatments their doctors prescribed but insurers rejected. Mark Cuban is among the company's backers, having previously intervened directly in a high-profile denial case before channelling support to Claimable's platform. The underlying dynamic Claimable exploits is that most Americans do not challenge denials despite a meaningful reversal rate for those who do; AI lowers the effort barrier by generating clinically grounded appeals automatically. For insurers and claims departments, the story represents an emerging adversarial AI dynamic: the same technology insurers are deploying to automate claims adjudication is now being used systematically to contest their decisions. As AI-generated appeals scale, insurers will face pressure to justify denial logic in greater detail — which will in turn require them to make their own AI decision systems more auditable and explainable.
