Hiro's product — which integrates users' credit cards, savings, and investment accounts to model scenarios and deliver tailored guidance — will shut down April 20, but its capabilities and ~10-person team are being folded directly into ChatGPT to build what founder Ethan Bloch called an "AI personal CFO." ### Anthropic's Mythos AI Forces Emergency Summit Between Bessent, Powell, and Bank CEOs
**Source:** [PYMNTS](https://www.pymnts.com/cybersecurity/2026/banks-face-complex-cyber-risks-from-anthropics-mythos/)  •  **Date:** April 10, 2026

Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent and Fed Chair Jerome Powell convened an urgent meeting on April 8 with CEOs of Citigroup, Bank of America, Morgan Stanley, Wells Fargo, and Goldman Sachs to brief them on cybersecurity risks posed by Anthropic's new Claude Mythos model — an AI system designed to detect software vulnerabilities, including 30-year-old flaws that no human auditor had previously discovered. The concern: Mythos could be weaponised to find and exploit zero-day vulnerabilities in the interconnected legacy systems banks still depend on, with the potential to destabilise institutions and customer access to funds. The day after the meeting, Anthropic launched Project Glasswing, providing early access to a Mythos preview for select firms including Amazon, JPMorgan Chase, Goldman Sachs, and CrowdStrike, so they can harden their systems before broader release. The episode marks a new chapter in systemic AI risk — where offensive AI capabilities, not just operational failures, become a top-tier financial stability concern.

### Visa Launches Intelligent Commerce Connect — Giving AI Agents a Formal Path to Pay
**Source:** [American Banker / PaymentsSource](https://www.americanbanker.com/payments/news/visa-mastercard-expand-agentic-ai-deployments)  •  **Date:** April 2–8, 2026

Visa officially launched its Intelligent Commerce developer toolkit and Digital Commerce Authentication Program, enabling AI agents to initiate secure, tokenised payments on behalf of users across Visa's network. Each agent receives a scoped token tied to spend controls set by the account holder; raw card data is never exposed, and tokens can be revoked individually if an agent is compromised. More than 30 partners are building in the sandbox, 20+ agent platforms are integrating directly, and hundreds of agent-initiated transactions have already been completed in production. Separately, Visa partnered with fintech Ramp to automate corporate bill payments via AI agents. Mastercard moved in parallel, expanding its agentic commerce network to Hong Kong — completing its first in-market agentic transaction via HSBC and a ride-share booking. The twin moves transform the payments infrastructure layer from passive rail to active identity-and-authorisation partner for the emerging agent economy.

### Primitive Launches AI Agent Operating System Built for Regulated Banks
**Source:** [PYMNTS](https://www.pymnts.com/artificial-intelligence-2/2026/primitive-launches-system-to-help-financial-institutions-deploy-ai-agents/)  •  **Date:** April 14, 2026

Primitive, founded by banking and fintech executive Derek White and backed by Fin Capital and Pelion Venture Partners, launched what it describes as the first AI agent operating system purpose-built for regulated financial institutions. The platform handles the full lifecycle — creating, deploying, governing, and measuring AI agents across operations — with bank-grade guardrails embedded from the start rather than bolted on. A launch partnership with MX Technologies (serving 1,700 financial institutions) will deploy a Growth Agent to help banks and credit unions identify and act on customer opportunities intelligently. White positioned the product around a core industry tension: "The challenge isn't access to AI — it's integrating, governing and proving the return on it at enterprise scale." Primitive joins a rapidly forming category of BFSI-specific AI orchestration infrastructure, alongside Oracle's agent platform and nCino's Agentic Operating System, as banks acknowledge that general-purpose AI tooling lacks the audit trail, explainability, and model-risk governance regulators require.

### nCino's Analyst Digital Partner Cuts Commercial Credit Review Time by Up to 70%
**Source:** [GlobeNewswire / nCino](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/04/06/3268360/0/en/nCino-Analyst-Digital-Partner-Cuts-Commercial-Relationship-Review-Time-by-Up-to-70-Enabling-Financial-Institutions-to-Build-an-AI-Augmented-Credit-Workforce.html)  •  **Date:** April 6, 2026

nCino published production results for its Analyst Digital Partner — a role-based AI agent trained on 14+ years of banking-specific data that works alongside human credit professionals to handle the analytical workload of commercial relationship reviews. Early adopters are reducing review effort by 60–70%, compressing timelines from 2–7 days to hours; one enterprise bank went live in 36 minutes. The tool also supports mortgage underwriting via AUS Smart Tasks and Document Validation, enabling income verification without manual effort and converting Automated Underwriting System findings into clear action steps. The broader capability sits within nCino's "dual workforce" vision: AI agents handle spreading, covenant testing, and documentation; human bankers redirect time to client relationships and judgment-intensive credit decisions. With regulators scrutinising model risk in underwriting, nCino's 14-year training corpus and built-in explainability are meaningful differentiators for institutions that cannot afford a black-box underwriting layer.
